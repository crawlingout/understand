Dear language learner,

Do you struggle to understand native speakers despite the fact that you already know some grammar and vocabulary? And how about movies - do you still need subtitles?

Language is often treated as knowledge that can be learned the same way we learn chemistry but it's above all a skill... and any skill has to be acquired through practice.

For example, in order to understand a movie or a natural conversation you need the ability to recognize words in sounds of fast uninterrupted speech and understand the meaning directly without translating in your head. You can't learn this from textbooks. But you can acquire this skill by using authentic audio content to practice.
